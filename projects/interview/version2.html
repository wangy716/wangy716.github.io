Interview with Shravani

Option 1 Option 3

Evelyn

Why did you choose communication design as your major?

Shravani

When it came to choosing an art field to work within, I knew I was very drawn towards typography and visual culture as a whole. On top of that, I really enjoyed the diverse subfields that are offered in this major. Between UX/UI design to editorial and brand-based projects, there are so many exciting opportunities I am keen to explore

Evelyn

Is there anything you will always inspire from or given a muse for you to create/design?

Shravani

I find a lot of inspiration in the past and nature. I really enjoy research, especially historical and scientific investigations, and doing so is a big part of my creative process. I like having a nice set of notes to peruse while trying to develop a design idea.

Evelyn

Do you have any people to look forward to?

Shravani

I am really inspired by many designers and artists, but I am especially drawn to the work and processes of Terry Gilliam, Shona Heath, Paula Scher, and Satyajit Ray. They are all creatives who have discussed their work styles in very candid ways that I found myself relating to and even emulating. On top of that, they are all based in fields (graphic design, editorial art direction, animation) that I highly enjoy and would love to delve into. Each individual designer has an immensely diverse body of work and I really admire their ability to explore such a wide range of projects.

Evelyn

What is your goal after three years of communication design study?

Shravani

Wow, that is a difficult question. I think I want to look back at my schooling and feel satisfied with making the most out of the opportunities I have received. I want to have a better grasp of the specific fields I want to work in and feel more confident about my personal creative opinions.

Evelyn

What other medium arts have you done?

Shravani

Honestly, I didn't immensely care for working in a digital space too much before beginning college. I really enjoyed painting, pen and ink, and pastels, and still try to use them in my side projects. But after my first year, I learned that I actually really enjoyed working in 3D with wire and metal, and creating animated and still-image films. I am glad that the first year of college gave me the chance to experiment with all of these different mediums and styles.

Evelyn

In general, what do you think art has brought to you? (either positive and negative)

Shravani

Wow, that's also a really good question! I think working in art and design has just been such an overarching aspect of my life and I find it (positively) seeping into many other aspects of living. By developing my creative process, I found that I was able to get a better grasp on writing essays, reading and analyzing texts, and just solving day-to-day problems. I must say that art has also brought me quite a lot of stress and very little sleep. It can get difficult to balance fearlessness with a desire to reach 'perfection' and that can result in a lot of frustration and burnout.

Evelyn

How do you define art?

Shravani

To me, art is a crossroads between making and expression. It's a really fascinating way of producing something from within and putting it out for others to see.

Evelyn

Besides art, what would you like to do?

Shravani

Besides art, I really love to cook. It's my number one way to destress and I love that by the end of it, I have something that can be used and enjoyed. I especially enjoy cooking for others, even though it's a bit tough in my tiny New York kitchen now. I find myself going through the creative process in cooking and baking too, so I guess that's another way art has positively impacted me.

Evelyn

What is your ideal dream life? To be given a day to live perfectly, how would you plan out?

Shravani

An ideal lifestyle for me would entail a nice work/life balance. I tend to focus too much on work and then experience burnout, which can be pretty irritating. I would really love to eradicate my procrastination/desire for perfection and give myself enough time to sleep and have fun.

Evelyn

What do you most believe in for life?

Shravani

I honestly don't know. I guess I just really value the power of experience? I don't know if I have a solid amount of faith in really anything, but I do admire learning about others and perspectives outside of my own. I just like that process of learning and the realization of how different other people/experiences can be. I don't know if that really answers the question, but it's the closest I can think of to truly believing in something.
